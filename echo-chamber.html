Echo Chamber

A 3D Unity narrative game — navigate three zones, place memory boxes on the correct platforms, and uncover the story.

Unity C# 3D Design Narrative Audacity Mixamo

About the Project

Echo Chamber is a 3D narrative-driven game built in Unity using C#. The player navigates through three distinct zones, each with its own atmosphere and challenges. In each zone, memory boxes must be placed on the correct platforms — and when placed correctly, each box reveals a piece of the game's storyline, gradually unfolding a narrative as you progress.

The game features 11 memory boxes across the three zones, a four-camera system, Mixamo character animations, and sound design edited in Audacity using audio sourced from Freesound. Textures were sourced from Pixabay and applied across all three environments.

The Three Zones

🔵

Zone 1

Blue circuit-patterned walls, an icy white floor, and warm-lit memory boxes. The opening zone that introduces the game's mechanics and narrative.

🔥

Zone 2

A fiery orange environment with spike traps, glowing particles, and wooden platforms. A more challenging zone with greater obstacles to navigate.

🟣

Zone 3

A dark, atmospheric room with deep blue circuit walls, a red glowing ceiling, and glowing blue platforms. The most intense and narrative-rich zone.

How to Play

  • Navigate through each of the three zones using your character
  • Find the memory boxes scattered across each zone
  • Place each memory box on the correct matching platform
  • When placed correctly, the box reveals a piece of the storyline
  • Collect all 11 memory boxes across the three zones to complete the narrative
  • Avoid obstacles like spike traps and barriers as you progress

Development Challenges

Designing three distinct zones

Each zone needed its own unique atmosphere and visual identity while still feeling like part of the same game. Sourcing the right Pixabay textures and lighting each zone differently was key to making them feel distinct.

Memory box placement and narrative triggers

Writing the C# logic to detect when a memory box was placed on the correct platform and then trigger the right piece of narrative text required careful scripting of collider triggers and game state tracking across 11 boxes.

Four-camera system

Building and switching between four cameras in Unity required understanding Unity's camera management system and scripting smooth transitions so the player always had the best view of the action.

Sound design

Editing and layering audio clips in Audacity to match the tone of each zone — from the eerie tech atmosphere of Zone 1 to the intense fire environment of Zone 2 — required careful timing and audio balancing.

What I Learned

Echo Chamber was my most ambitious Unity project to date. It pushed me to think about game design holistically — not just the mechanics, but the narrative, atmosphere, sound, and visual design all working together. I developed strong skills in C# scripting, Unity's camera and UI systems, and asset management across a multi-zone environment. I also learned how to research and apply game studies theory to my own design decisions, referencing theorists like Jenkins, Dourish, Bogost, and Flanagan to underpin the design choices in Echo Chamber.
